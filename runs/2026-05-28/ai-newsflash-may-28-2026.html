Apple iOS 27 opens Siri to Claude, Gemini, and Grok — ChatGPT exclusivity ends this fall
Apple confirmed this week that iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 will introduce an AI Extensions framework letting users choose their preferred AI model for Siri, Writing Tools, and Image Playground. Google, Anthropic, and xAI are expected to add Extensions support to their existing iOS apps. Once installed, a single toggle in Settings routes all Apple Intelligence requests to the chosen model. Different AI voices can be assigned to different tasks — Apple's own voice for on-device queries, Claude's voice for responses requiring external knowledge. iOS 27 will be formally introduced at WWDC on June 8.

Over 1.5 billion active Apple devices become distribution surface for every major AI lab simultaneously. The lab that earns the default slot for the most common query types wins a reach no amount of advertising can match. This reshapes the consumer AI race entirely.

SubQ launches the first commercial non-transformer model — 12M token context, $29M seed
Subquadratic launched SubQ on May 5 with $29 million in seed funding, introducing what it claims is the first commercially available model built entirely on a non-transformer architecture. SubQ features a 12-million token context window — the largest context of any model currently available — enabled by its linear-scaling architecture that doesn't suffer from the quadratic attention cost that limits transformer context lengths. The model is designed for tasks requiring deep long-document analysis: legal review, scientific literature synthesis, full-codebase reasoning, and financial document processing. Benchmarks against transformer-based models of comparable parameter counts are pending independent verification.
Why it mattersTransformers have been the unchallenged foundation of every major language model since 2017. If SubQ's architecture scales, it threatens to obsolete the hardware stacks, training pipelines, and inference infrastructure that the entire industry — including NVIDIA — has been built around. A 12M token context is not an incremental improvement; it's an architectural statement that the transformer era may have a ceiling.

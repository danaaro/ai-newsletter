NVIDIA launches Ising — the world's first open AI models for quantum computing
NVIDIA released Ising, a family of open-source AI models purpose-built to accelerate quantum computing. The family includes two components: Ising Calibration (a 35-billion-parameter vision-language model that analyzes quantum processor experiment output and automates calibration with minimal human oversight) and Ising Decoding (two models tuned for speed and accuracy in real-time quantum error correction). Ising Decoding is 2.5× faster and 3× more accurate than traditional approaches. The models integrate with CUDA-Q, NVIDIA's hybrid quantum-classical computing platform. Early adopters include Harvard, Fermilab, and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.
Why it mattersQuantum computing's biggest bottleneck isn't the qubits — it's the software layer that calibrates and corrects them. NVIDIA just open-sourced that layer. A 35B parameter model doing real-time error correction could compress years off the path to fault-tolerant quantum computers.

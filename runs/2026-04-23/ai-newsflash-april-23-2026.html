Sony's Ace robot beats pro table tennis players — published in Nature, a milestone for physical AI
Sony AI's Project Ace — an autonomous robot using an eight-jointed arm on a mobile base — defeated elite human table tennis players in real competitive matches this week, with the research published in Nature. Ace uses event-based vision sensors for high-speed perception and model-free reinforcement learning for real-time control, tracking both the ball's position and spin. In December 2025 and March 2026 validation matches against professional and elite players, Ace won the majority of contests. The system operates entirely autonomously — no human input during play.
Why it mattersA Nature publication on a robot defeating professional humans in a real-time physical sport is a landmark. This isn't a controlled lab demo — it's competitive play in the real world, under pressure, against experts. The perception and control systems developed for Ace have direct applications to manufacturing, surgery, logistics, and any domain requiring precision physical interaction in dynamic environments.
